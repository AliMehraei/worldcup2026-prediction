Prediction Model — Dixon-Coles Method

The Dixon-Coles model (1997) is the academic gold standard for football prediction — used by Opta, club analytics teams across Europe, and probabilistic forecasters. Goals follow a Poisson distribution with team-specific attack (α) and defense (β) parameters, corrected for low-scoring result bias via the ρ coefficient.

Step 1 · Strength Params
Attack & Defense Ratings
Each team gets α (attack strength) and β (defensive weakness) calibrated from Elo + FIFA ranking + recent form over 24 months.
α_i = exp(attack_i)
β_j = exp(defense_j)
γ = venue_advantage
Step 2 · Expected Goals
Per-match λ Calculation
Expected goal rate (λ) computed for each team in each specific fixture — the mean of the Poisson distribution for that team.
λ_home = α_home × β_away × γ
λ_away = α_away × β_home
Step 3 · Score Distribution
Dixon-Coles Correction ρ
Each scoreline (x–y) probability computed from independent Poisson distributions, then corrected with ρ for low-scoring results (0-0, 1-0, 0-1, 1-1).
P(x,y) = Pois(x,λ_h) × Pois(y,λ_a)
× τ(x,y,λ_h,λ_a,ρ)
Step 4 · Monte Carlo
10,000 Full Simulations
The entire tournament is simulated 10,000 times from group stage to final. Champion probabilities = share of simulations won by each team.
P(champion=T) =
count(sim: winner==T) / 10000
