Model & Methodology

Dixon-Coles bivariate Poisson — the same academic approach used by Opta, FiveThirtyEight, and professional club analytics teams.

📖
Why Dixon-Coles?

Mark Dixon and Stuart Coles published their landmark 1997 paper establishing the bivariate Poisson framework as the most accurate method for modelling football match outcomes. Unlike simple Elo-based systems, Dixon-Coles generates a full probability distribution over every possible scoreline, enabling precise expected goal calculations and tournament simulation.

Step 1 · Strength Parameters
Attack & Defense Ratings
Each team gets α (attack strength) and β (defensive weakness) calibrated from Elo + FIFA ranking + recent form. Higher α = more goals expected. Lower β = fewer goals conceded.
α_i = exp(attack_i) β_j = exp(defense_j) γ = venue_advantage_factor
Step 2 · Expected Goals λ
Per-match λ Calculation
Expected goal rate λ (Poisson mean) computed for each team in each specific fixture. λ represents the average number of goals a team will score in this exact matchup.
λ_home = α_home × β_away × γ λ_away = α_away × β_home (neutral: γ = 1.0)
Step 3 · Score Probabilities
Poisson Distribution
Each scoreline (x–y) probability computed from independent Poisson distributions. Sum across all (x,y) gives P(home win), P(draw), P(away win).
P(X=x) = (e^-λ × λ^x) / x! P(score=x,y) = Pois(x, λ_h) × Pois(y, λ_a)
Step 4 · Dixon-Coles Fix
ρ Correction for Low Scores
Standard Poisson over-predicts 0-0, 1-0, 0-1, and 1-1 outcomes. The τ(ρ) correction factor adjusts these four scorelines to match empirical football data. ρ = −0.08.
τ(x,y,λ_h,λ_a,ρ): (0,0): 1 - λ_h×λ_a×ρ (1,0): 1 + λ_a×ρ (0,1): 1 + λ_h×ρ (1,1): 1 - ρ else: 1
Step 5 · Tournament Sim
10,000 Monte Carlo Runs
The entire WC2026 tournament — all 104 matches from group stage to final — is simulated 10,000 times independently. Each run draws from the Poisson distributions.
for sim in range(10000): run_group_stage() run_r32_through_final() record_outcomes()
Step 6 · Aggregation
Probability Estimates
Championship probability = fraction of simulations a team wins. R32 probability = fraction where they qualify from groups. All tournament-path probabilities computed the same way.
P(champion=T) = count(sim: winner==T) / 10000 P(R32=T) = count(sim: T qualifies) / 10000
⚖️
Input Weights
How λ is calibrated

The λ parameters are not computed from Elo alone. Multiple signals are blended with the following weights:

ELO
World Football Elo Rating (historically calibrated, back to 1990)
35%
FIFA
Official FIFA/Coca-Cola World Ranking
25%
FORM
Last 12 months results with time-decay weighting (recent matches weighted more)
15%
SQUAD
Squad quality proxy — Transfermarkt total market value
12%
HIST
World Cup tournament history 2006–2022 (rounds reached, xG performance)
8%
H2H
Direct head-to-head record + tactical style matchup adjustment
5%
⚠️
Model Limitations
Limitation 1
Injury & Squad Availability
The model cannot account for last-minute injuries to key players. Losing Mbappé or Messi to injury would significantly alter France/Argentina's probabilities.
Limitation 2
In-Tournament Form
λ values are static — they don't update as the tournament progresses. A team that dominates the group stage doesn't get a λ boost for the knockouts.
Limitation 3
Penalty Shootout
Penalties are modelled as a 50/50 tiebreak with slight Elo adjustment, but real penalty performance (dedicated practice, specialist kickers) is not captured.
Limitation 4
Proxy Data
Some lower-ranked teams (FIFA 50–85) use estimated Elo values marked with *. Direct data availability is limited for these teams.
